Nagpur Maha Metro

Nagpur Metro is a modern rapid transit system that serves the city of Nagpur in Maharashtra, India. It consists of two main lines: the Orange Line (North-South Corridor) and the Aqua Line (East-West Corridor), covering a total of 37 stations across a 38.2 km network. The metro system was inaugurated on March 8, 2019, and is operated by the Maharashtra Metro Rail Corporation Limited. The trains, comprising three coaches each, run at a top speed of 90 km/h, with an average speed of 33 km/h and a frequency of every 10 minutes, ensuring efficient service. The system is powered by 25 kV AC overhead catenary electrification, contributing to its sustainability efforts.
